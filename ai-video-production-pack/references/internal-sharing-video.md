# Internal Sharing / Explainer Video

Use for company sharing, methodology explanation, AI workflow demos, PLO/OpenClaw/CER/PTR videos.

## Tone

Clear, credible, lightly impressive. Avoid over-viral drama unless the user asks.

## Structure

1. Hook: one strong question or contrast.
2. Problem: fragmented work / lost context / no feedback loop.
3. Framework: PLO or system architecture.
4. Mechanism: agents, memory, workflow, dashboard, automations.
5. Real case: CER/PTR or current project.
6. Meta reveal: the video was produced by the system.
7. Takeaway: one sentence the audience remembers.

## Visual Strategy

- Prefer light-tech, clean dashboards, glass cards, soft gradients, subtle grid.
- Use real screenshots for proof; use AI video for transitions/metaphors.
- Use motion graphics for exact diagrams.
- Keep text overlays minimal: 2–6 Chinese characters or one short English phrase.

## Prompt Pattern

```text
16:9 clean light-tech internal presentation style. [central visual metaphor].
Camera: [slow push / parallax / top-down timeline / node orbit].
Motion: controlled, elegant, no chaotic particles.
Palette: white, soft blue, pale cyan, dark slate text, subtle glow.
No cyberpunk, no dark neon, no unreadable text, no distorted UI.
```

## Review Gates

- Does the scene advance the business idea?
- Would a colleague trust this, not just think it is flashy?
- Are proof screenshots separated from generated metaphors?
- Is the motion calm enough for internal sharing?
